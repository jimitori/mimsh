Avant Garde Glagolitic

A fusion of the ancient Glagolitic script with the revolutionary aesthetics of Russian avant-garde movements—Suprematism, Constructivism, Rayism, and abstractionism—uniting two Slavic-born traditions of bold human invention.

Goals

To explore and synthesize the graphic originality of Glagolitic script with the radical visual language of early 20th-century Slavic avant-garde art, creating a new typographic expression.

My Role

Conceptualization, research, design, and creation of a unique alphabet blending historical and modernist influences.

Duration

Ongoing

Concept & Process

The Avant Garde Glagolitic project marries the ancient Glagolitic script—a Slavic creation from the 9th century—with the dynamic, abstract forms of early 20th-century avant-garde movements like Suprematism and Constructivism. Both emerged from Slavic cultural contexts, making them natural companions in this exploration. My process involved studying the geometric purity of Glagolitic letterforms and reinterpreting them through the lens of abstractionism, prioritizing bold shapes and deliberate invention.

Results

This ongoing project has so far produced over 12 characters, each a distinctive fusion of Glagolitic heritage and avant-garde innovation. It celebrates the enduring spirit of Slavic creativity while pushing the boundaries of traditional typography. Though still in progress, I’m considering compiling the results into a functional font.
