About Me

Bridging the worlds of design, linguistics, and accessibility, Mitia Morovov-Sheiner crafts not just visuals, but systems of meaningâ€”whether in digital interfaces, experimental typography, or constructed languages. His work is driven by a fascination with how we communicate, shaping experiences that are both functional and thought-provoking.

As a PhD linguist, Mitia explores writing systems, sign languages, and language evolution. He is the creator of Ophidian, a language inspired by serpent-like phonetics and rhythm. His passion for language extends to his reels about linguistics, where he shares insights into rare scripts, phonetics, and the structure of human communication.

As an autistic individual with ADHD and color blindness, Mitia approaches creativity through a distinct cognitive lens. He sees these traits not as limitations but as powerful tools that shape his work in unexpected ways. A cosmopolitan and future optimist, he values ideas over ideologies and constantly seeks to push boundaries in design, linguistics, and accessibility.
